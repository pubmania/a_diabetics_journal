    Tomato Keftedes (Fried Santorini Tomato Balls / Tomato Fritters)

    While the original recipe uses flour, I replaced it with the combination of wheat gluten and almond flour and it turned out great.

    Tastes great with Tomato Mint Pepper Spinach Dip, Tzatziki Dip and Basil Mint Pepper Garlic Dip.

    Danger

    This recipe is based on wheat gluten so it is not suitable for those with Gluten Intolerance. I am sure it's obvious but still.

    • Steps¶

      • Step 1: Cut the 160 gms (4 medium) tomatoes using knife into small pieces.
      • Step 2: Place chopped tomatoes in a bowl with 1/2 tsp salt and leave them for 30 minutes.
      • Step 3: Finely chop 40 gms (1 small) red onion and add it to the bowl.
      • Step 4: Add the oregano (to taste), salt (to taste), freshly ground black pepper (to taste) to the bowl.
      • Step 5: Mix the contents of bowl with a spatula
      • Step 6: Take 100 gms feta cheese and mash it using a fork and add it to the above mix.
      • Step 7: Add 1/4 cup vital wheat gluten, 1/4 cup almond flour and 1 tsp baking powder.
      • Step 8: Using spatula keep mixing the contents of the bowl until the ingredients are combined and the mixture is firm enough to make the balls. Add a little more vital wheat gluten (if needed).
      • Step 9: Put in the fridge for 30 minutes.
      • Step 10: Now apply 1/2 tsp extra virgin olive oil on palms and make 16 balls from the dough.
      • Step 11: Flatten the balls into cutlet shape.
      • Step 12: Preheat the air fryer to 200°C.
      • Step 13: Once hot, spread 1.5 tbsp extra virgin olive oil in the air fryer basket.
      • Step 14: Then place the flattened balls in the air fryer basket and cook for 12-15 minutes at 200°C.
      • Step 15: Serve hot.
